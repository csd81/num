================================================================================
SPECIFIC ALGORITHMIC IMPROVEMENTS
================================================================================

Gaussian Elimination Methods:
  - No-pivoting version: Fast but unstable without good conditioning
  - Partial pivoting: Stable, reduces rounding errors
  - Complete pivoting: Most stable, requires tracking variable permutations
  - Gauss-Jordan: Direct RREF computation, useful for matrix inverse

Iterative Methods:
  - Jacobi: Parallelizable but slower convergence
  - Gauss-Seidel: Sequential but typically faster convergence
  - Both now show spectral radius for convergence analysis

Interpolation Methods:
  - Lagrange/Vandermonde: Simple, ill-conditioned for large n
  - Newton Divided Differences: More stable, efficient evaluation
  - Hermite: Includes derivative constraints
  - Cubic Splines: Smooth piecewise polynomials with C^2 continuity

Integration Methods:
  - Trapezoidal: O(n^-2) error, simple and robust
  - Simpson: O(n^-4) error, much better for smooth functions
  - Both use compositing for accuracy
